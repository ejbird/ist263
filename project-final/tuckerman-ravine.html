Tuckerman Ravine

The birthplace of backcountry skiing, Tuckerman Ravine has long been celebrated by skiers. Perched on Mount Washington, Tuckerman Ravine is located on the tallest mountain east of the Missisippi. Aside from altitude, Tuck's benefits from its location, as Mount Washington is known to be a weather anomaly. Carrying the title for the worlds worst weather. A trip to Tuckerman Ravine is never to be forgotten. Offering views usually associated with western mountains, visiting Tuckerman Ravine is a great way to enjoy a taste of the big mountain experience in an area otherwise devoid of these features.

Location and Weather Information

Trailhead Parking is located roughly 10 miles outside of Gorham, NH. The Tuckerman Ravine trail takes you 3 miles up to hermit lake where you get your first good glimpse of the bowl. From Hermit Lake, there is another 1 mile to the top of the bowl.

Note: Weather on Mount Washington changes rapidly. Prepare for all conditions. Mountain weather likely will be different than the Gorham forecast

Crowding:

Tuckerman Ravine is a well-known trail and highly traffickled. It is advised to start early to beat crowds. Although Tuckerman's is crowded, it earns one of the first feature on this site because of its beauty and ability to hold snow until the summer.
Crowd Rating: 3/10

Wildlife Information

Tuckerman Ravine's unique environment means the wildlife is radically different than other areas nearby. One rare Canada Lynx calls Mount Washington home and is the only one in the area.
